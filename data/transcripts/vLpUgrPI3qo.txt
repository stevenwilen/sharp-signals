This is how Topuria knocked out
Holloway. After Topuria threw, Holloway
would often square his stance to circle
out quickly.
This would happen even with long jabs.
Topuria reads and tests this by feinting
a jab, then throwing a lead hook, which
confirmed Holloway would square then
move away from the hook.
To avoid becoming predictable, Topuria
then punishes on the opposite side,
extending his combo with a left hook
after Holloway squares and circles away
from an overhand, which rocks him.
With his read solidified, Topuria feints
a jab to square Holloway, feints a left
hook to bait Holloway to move to the
opposite side without committing to a
movement, then goes all in on the now
perfectly lined up overhand to create
the beginning of the end.
