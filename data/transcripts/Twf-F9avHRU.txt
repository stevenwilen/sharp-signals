Mizuki Ino made yet another comeback

321. I say yet another because this was
her first fight after 2 years of

years on the sidelines. So who is this
inactive fighter buried on the early
premiums and why should you care? Let's
rewind a little. I first saw Misuki over

Graasso back at in big TFC 11. The very

watching those two young girls, Mizukia

seeing the future of women's MMA. They

technical foundation, the athleticism,
and most importantly, the composure that
made me hopeful for the future of the
sport and especially for the strawway
division. Mizuki actually started
fighting professionally at just 15 years
old in kickboxing and later that same
year at 16 in MMA. She went on to
compile a 13 and5 record winning the
deep jewels straightway title and even
challenging Bernina and Drova for the
Invicta FC Strawway Championship before
eventually signing with the UFC. Still
only 25 at the time, Misuki made a

pounds and beat Wuan, who not only was

missed weight by 3 lbs. The COVID
pandemic scrapped her scheduled fight
against Tisha Torres, but less than a
year later, she dropped back down to 115
to face a rising Amanda Lemo, a tough
matchup that ended in a loss.
Unfortunately, she tore her ACL during
that fight with first surgery and a
three-year layoff. Mizuki returned in
2023 to defeat Hannah Goldie only to
disappear again for another two years.
This time there wasn't a clear reason
for the absence, but at UFC 321 opening
the prelims, Misuki finally made her
return and this time she delivered,
scoring a decisive win against
Jacqueline Amarim. And that brings us to
Misuki's fight against Jaceline Amarim.
Coming in, Msuki didn't have an easy
task. Amarim is an accomplished BJJ
black belt who after losing her UFC
debut went on a tear. Four finishes in
four fights. The athletic American top
team fighter was dominating on the
ground even against fellow grapplers.
Combine that with Mizuki's 2-year layoff
and the odds were clearly stacked in
Amarim's favor. She entered the fight as
a heavy favorite with odds ranging from
minus400 to -490 while Mizuki was listed
as a plus 330 to plus 380 underdog.
Let's get into the actual fight.
Mizuki fought out of a crouch low
stance, ready to defend takedowns and
use her boxing skills. She was active
with her footwork and faints, looking to
take the initiative away from the
Brazilian fighter. It seems like Ring Ro
was present here as she was having
trouble fighting her distance with her
hands initially and was probably careful
about overcommitting and being taken
down. Still, she kept the pressure on
Amarim, catching her kicks and rendering
her ineffective in open space. Slowly
she started finding counters, drawing
predictable shots with her pressure. Her
cross counter was of note. Halfway
through round one, Amarine shot for a
takedown and Chen successfully put
Mizuki down.
Mizuki initially used a butterfly hook
and a whizer to build base and get back
to her feet, but Amarine secured a rear
body lock, dragging her back to the mat
and taking the back in the process.
This was the danger zone, a checkmate
position for the Brazilian. Mizuki
defended the Paris positions well,
cycling between mount and back, trying
to escape without being submitted until
she fought off a hook and rotated to end
up inside the guard of the BJJ black
belt. Once Mizuki cleared a gift wrap
and a deep overhook, it was payback
time. She finished the round poster up
and landing damage from top position.
From round two on the fight dynamic
changed and Marim needed to be more

Issuki meanwhile fought with renowned

While Amor was willing to bite her

Msuka remained undeterred and confident,

Even when ending on bottom, she was
determined to break down Amarim, who

Round three continued the same pattern.
Mizuki hurt Amarim with body shots,

Why do I care? First, I'm just a fan. I
think Mizuki has a cool fighting style,
but this performance also gave me hope
for the future of WMA. Just like I felt
10 years ago watching her fight Alexa
Graasso, women's MMA might not have
developed the way I thought, there are
still cultural and structural barriers,
some that affect all fighters and some
that are exclusive to women. Many
fighter stories are about overcoming
these hardships. Mizuki was once the
prodigious drunk prospect and now she
was the better underdog coming off a
long layoff. Despite all of that, I
think she looked great. I'm hoping
Mizuki can stay active and make an
impact in the current strawway division.
maybe even fulfill the potential so many
saw in her 10 years ago. Funnily enough,

And who knows, there's a lot that goes
