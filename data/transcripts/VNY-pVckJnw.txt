Coming up on the 13th of June  
from the Desert Diamond Arena in Glendale, 
Arizona, in a championship bout that will be  
streamed only on DAZN - Jesse ‘Bam’ Rodriguez 
is moving up in weight to challenge the WBA  
Bantamweight champion Antonio Vargas. Having 
previously held world championships at both  
Flyweight and Junior Bantamweight, Bam is 
universally recognized as one of the very  
best P4P boxers competing in the world today.
Vargas will provide Bam with the opportunity to  
become a 3-division world champion, which would 
be quite the impressive feat for the 26 year old  
prizefighter. But perhaps more important still, 
a victory against Vargas would seemingly pave the  
way for Bam to have a big time showdown against 
the man widely recognized as THE P4P King in  
boxing today, the one and only Naoya Inoue.
Vargas is a very technical boxer, one who blends  
an aggressive pressure style with high volume 
punching and some very good infighting ability.  
He won the WBA interim belt when he scored a 10th 
round stoppage victory against Winston Guerrero  
back in December 2024. In May of last year he was 
promoted and became the real WBA champion - this  
happened after Seiya Tsutsumi was designated 
as champion in recess. Last time out in July  
of last year, Vargas defended the WBA title when 
he battled Daigo Higa to a 12 round majority draw.  
Late last year, however, Vargas was designated 
as champion in recess, and Tsutsumi was  
reinstated as champion. But he was medically 
sidelined yet again, and after some confusion,  
the WBA confirmed last month that Vargas is 
indeed the true WBA Bantamweight champion.
 
Bam is a brilliant southpaw with hypnotic footwork 
and an uncanny ability to discover favorable  
angles. After scoring a stoppage win against Sunny 
Edwards, his final Flyweight title defense back in  
December 2023, Bam went on a hell of a run when he 
returned to 115. First he outgunned Juan Francisco  
Estrada to score a knockout and win back the 
WBC Junior Bantamweight title that previously  
belonged to him. He successfully defended that 
title when he made fairly quick work of Pedro  
Guevara. After that, last July, Bam methodically 
wore down Cafu to win by 10th round stoppage,  
and that was a unification bout where Bam 
added the WBO belt. And then last November,  
Bam had another unification bout, winning by 
10th round knockout against Fernando Martinez,  
where he added the WBA to his existing 
unified championship collection.
That is where we stand, with Bam vacating his 
throne to move up. It is one of the situations  
where the challenger moving up to make his debut 
at a new weight class is the heavy favorite  
to win, and the reigning champion accustomed 
to the weight is generally being overlooked,  
if not outright dismissed. To be sure, Vargas has 
real talent, he is a very clever operator in tight  
quarters, he knows how to apply pressure, and he 
is good at making midfight adjustments. Vargas is  
a smart fighter with a nifty bag of tricks, 
and it would be foolish to write him off.
All the same, Bam is considered among 
the very best boxers in the world today,  
and for damn good reason. Ever since Nakatani 
moved up, I have a difficult time imagining anyone  
south of Junior Featherweight beating Bam right 
now. Beyond that, Vargas uses that aggressive  
style that often leaves him open, meaning - his 
strategy inherently comes with the territory of  
making himself a bit vulnerable at times, and I 
think sooner or later, that will spell disaster  
against a stellar counterpuncher like Bam.
For my official prediction, I am taking Bam  
Rodriguez by 11th round stoppage. Simply put, I 
believe Bam is operating on another level here,  
and I believe it will show. So I like Bam 
by late stoppage, but as always, what the  
hell do I know? I ain’t exactly Quasimodo over 
here. But I am extremely fired up for this one,  
and I am very interested in seeing how Bam looks 
at the new weight - because I think how well he  
performs in this one may give us some indication 
about what his chances are in a would-be fight  
against Inoue. If Bam looks really good at 
the weight, then he will carry some positive  
momentum into a showdown against The Monster. But 
if he struggles at the weight, where his power,  
ability to take a punch, or his stamina seem 
subpar, that’s probably a bad sign, suggesting  
a showdown with Inoue is ill-advised. Whatever 
the case may be, I can’t wait to find out!
