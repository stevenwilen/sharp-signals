This fight isn't just about who holds
the belt. It's about who gets the
respect and recognition as one of the
best fighters in the world
pound-for-pound. Chel Sunen said it
best. If Duplexes beats Chimayv, he's
flirting with the top three
pound-for-pound fighters on the planet.
That's a huge jump, especially
considering how brutal the middleweight
division has become. Sonnen calls it the
era of Anderson Silva is gone, replaced
by a division stacked with killers. Only
a handful can break through to that
elite tier. For Chimayv, a win would
cement him as the undisputed force at
middleweight and launch him into the
pound-for-pound conversation overnight.
But a loss wouldn't just be a setback.
It would raise questions about his
stamina, adaptability, and whether his
rapid rise was a flash in the pan. For
Duplexes, a win solidifies him as one of
the UFC's fiercest champions. It means
overcoming a formidable wrestling
nightmare and proving his striking and
endurance can withstand elite pressure.
It opens doors to super fights, bigger
paydays, and that coveted top three
pound-for-pound ranking.
