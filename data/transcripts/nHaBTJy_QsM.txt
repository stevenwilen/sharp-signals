refers to a phenomenon where male UFC
fighters aged 35 and older competing in
weight classes from flyweight up to
welterweight have an overwhelmingly poor
record in title fights. In the UFC's
men's divisions below welterweight,
fighters aged 35 and up went 2 and 31 in
championship bouts for several years,
effectively creating a statistical curse
that suggested that they were unlikely
to win. The reason is because fighters
aed 35 and older often begin to
experience declines in factors crucial
at lower weight classes, speed, reaction
time, recovery rate, and cardio. Because
flyweight through lightweight bouts
emphasize pace and volume striking, even
a slight deterioration in reflexes can
be decisive. While heavyweights and
light heavyweights sometimes enjoy
success beyond 35, given size and
strength tend to decline more slowly,
lighter divisions amplify age related
disadvantages, reinforcing the curse
stigma. But on April 12th, 2025,
Alexander Vulcanowski, who was 36 years
old, won the vacant featherweight title
by unanimous decision over Diego Lopez.
This victory marked the first time a
fighter aged 35 or older in any men's
division from 125 to 155 secured a UFC
championship. officially ending the
