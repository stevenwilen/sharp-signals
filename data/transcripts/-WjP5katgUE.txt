Let's look at Gane's takedown defense
against Jones. Gane overextends on a
cross, which gives Jones the body lock
in his back. He tries to turn into
Jones, but it was too late, and he
should have been hand fighting and
getting to the fence. He then gives up
the trip in the takedown. Against
Spivac, Gane was better about managing
distance and forced Spivac to shoot from
far away, which gave him time to defend.
Against Volkov, Gane wasn't resetting
quickly enough after using his legs. So,
Volkov turned these moments into
takedowns. So, generally, Gane's
striking mistakes set up his opponent's
takedowns.
