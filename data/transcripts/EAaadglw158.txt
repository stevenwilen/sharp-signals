Valentina Chevchenko's second reign is
proof you can reboot greatness. At UFC
306, she silenced the Alexa Graasso
trilogy with a decisive win. At UFC 315,
she defended against Man and Furo,
showing that at 37, her timing, her
trips, her counter lefts are still
sharp. Chevchenko fights like a clock
that always keeps time. Every step has a
purpose. Every scramble, she's first.
She's the metronome with a blade. Jeang
Lee has been nothing short of dominant
at strawweight. Since regaining her belt
in 2022, she's defended three times
against Amanda Lamos, Yan Shaan, and
most recently Tatiana Suarez at UFC 312.
10 UFC wins, five bonuses, and a
reputation for raw horsepower paired
with better composure than ever. Now at
36, she's vacating her belt to climb
into Valentina's world. If she wins, she
becomes only the second woman after
Amanda Nunez to capture belts in two
divisions. Those fans have been calling
for this matchup for years. Now it's
here. So what happens when the storm
hits the clock? Valentina's keys punish
urgency with the counter left, dictate
pace, and bank rounds with trips. Jang's
keys. Cut the cage, tax the legs, and
turn every exit into a clinch. The swing
