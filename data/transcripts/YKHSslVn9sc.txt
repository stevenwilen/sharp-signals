Drew Dober just reminded everybody why
you do not stand in front of that man
too long. Michael Johnson was looking
sharp early, moving well, landing clean,
doing Michael Johnson things, and then
Drew found the button. Second round, one
clean left hand, and good night. Not a
flurry, not some messy referee save,
just one of those punches where your
soul briefly checks departure times.
That was vintage Drew Dober. Pressure,
patience, and then pure violence. And
with that second round TKO at UFC 326,
he adds another knockout to a resume
that already had plenty of bad memories
on it for other lightweights. The scary
part?
Dober doesn't need a lot of space. He
doesn't need a long setup. Give him one
opening, one bad read, one lazy
exchange, and suddenly the fight night
doctor is asking very personal
questions.
