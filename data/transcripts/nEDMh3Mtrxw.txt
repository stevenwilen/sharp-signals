Losing Enanu was the worst mistake the
UFC has made in the heavyweight
division, maybe ever. Enanu wasn't just
a fighter. He was a walking highlight
reel, a global star, a guy who could
headline in any country on Earth. And
the UFC let him walk because they didn't
want to budge on pay and flexibility. So
now he's boxing Tyson Fury, potentially
fighting Deontay Wilder, signing with
PFL, and the UFC is left trying to
convince fans that Volkov versus Blades
2 is worth caring about. It's not just
about Francis himself. He was good for
the UFC, but he alone was not going to
save the division any more than Tom
will. It's about what he represented. He
was the myth, the monster, the
embodiment of the heavyweight aura.
Heavyweights should scare people the way
someone like Mark Coleman used to.
