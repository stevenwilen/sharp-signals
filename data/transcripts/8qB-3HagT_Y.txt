Shane Mosley Jr. was born December 18th,
1990 in Pomona, California. He is the
son of the Hall of Famer Sugar Shane
Mosley. He began training at age 16
under dad and grandfather Jack Mosley.
He participated in the show The
Contender and does some modeling.
Shane Mosley Jr.'s pros are that he's
got a good chin on him and he's taken
big shots from Jesus Ramos Jr. and Jason
Quigley and has never been knocked out
in his career. He's got a good jab and
one-two combinations. He's got his
genetics from his father Sugar Shane
Mosley, which benefits him as well.
His cons are that he's not a big puncher
and at times doesn't have enough for his
opponents to respect him in the ring.
His work rate is low at times and could
be a problem against the opposite of
Serhii. His defense is leaky and can be
a factor.
Serhii Bohachuk was born April 24th,
1995 in Vinnytsia, Ukraine. His nickname
is El Flaco. His head trainer is Manny
Robles and he is a family man.
Serhii Bohachuk's pros are that he's got
relentless work rate with a high
pressure style of fighting for all 12
rounds. He's a good inside fighter and
knows how to break down opponents. He's
skilled at shortening up shots to get
more leverage. He's got elite level
experience with the likes of Vergil
Ortiz Jr., Brandon Adams, Brian Mendoza,
and Butayev.
Serhii's cons are that he might have a
lot of mileage on him. There might be
concerns of wear and tear on his body
even though he is the younger fighter in
numerical years, but has been through a
lot of wars against elite level
competition and maybe slowing down a
tad.
Serhii Bohachuk's punch stats, according

22.8 and total punches per round at 75.6

pressure fighter and looks to have one

his opponent in every fight.
