Jack Catterall was born July 1st, 1993

Jack Catterall's pros are that he has
mastered the control of range and having
the discipline to make it a boring fight
at his opponent's expense as he's
constantly looking to counter and make
his opponent fight his fight. He's got a
good jab and enough power to keep the
opponents at bay and off of him to fight
at his range, but not a power puncher.
Jack has more experience in the
professional ranks with 15 more fights
all in the UK. This will be his first
fight outside of the UK since his debut
back in 2012. He also has 153 more
rounds fought in the pros. He relies on
counter-punching style and does a good
job of punching on his back foot.
Jack Catterall is known to have a low
work rate and is not a
television-friendly fighter. CompuBox
has him ranked in three categories in
the top 10 for fewest total punches
thrown per round, fewest power thrown
per round, and fewest power landed per
round. In a lot of exchanges in the
center of the ring, he has a bad habit
of having his head over his feet and
being unbalanced in which he gets
countered.
Jack Catterall's got decent pop on his
shots, but in no way a power puncher. A
lot of his shots are just hard enough to
keep his opponents at bay. Depending on
who the judges are in terms of a broad
scope, the optics of his style isn't
come forward and aggressive in which

Shakhram Giyasov, he was born July 7th,
1993 in Bukhara, Uzbekistan and
currently resides in Brooklyn, New York.
His nickname is Wonder Boy.
He has an Olympic silver medal in 2016
at the Rio Olympics at the welterweight
division. His trainer is Joel Diaz.
Joel Diaz has trained several other
Uzbekistan fighters such as Israil
Madrimov,
Akhmadaliev, Bektemir Melikuziev,
Jalolov,
Otabek Kholmatov,
and Ruslan Abdullaev.
Shakhram Giyasov has excellent footwork
with an Eastern European style pendulum
foot movement in which he is constantly
in and out of his opponent's range.
Giyasov has the better power in his
shots and looks to sit down on his
shots. He has a good active jab and
combines this with good hand speed,
feints, and level changes to have his
opponents constantly second-guessing
what to do. He can wear opponents down
even when he's not throwing shots. He's
had an excellent amateur pedigree and
some reports of over 300 amateur fights
and a silver medal in Rio.
Shakhram Giyasov's cons is that his pro

Catterall's. Even though he's 32, he

Giyasov has been inactive since April
12th, 2025, a little over a year, and

rust, especially with a fighter like
Catterall where he's constantly cagey

The reason for his layoff, according to
