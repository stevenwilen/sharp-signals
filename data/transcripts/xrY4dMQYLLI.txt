Every fight becomes less about whether

Win by decision,
people ask what went wrong.
Get taken down,
people act like the blueprint has been
discovered.
Don't finish a tough opponent, suddenly
the hype train needs maintenance.
That is the stupid economy of modern
fight fandom.
But Dakota seems to understand the
assignment. She knows people [music]
tune in for her striking. She knows her
image is tied to violence with polish.
She knows that fans expect the body
kicks, the knees, the pressure, the
finishes.
And there is nothing wrong with
embracing that.
The best fighters usually have a clear
identity. Remember Chuck Liddell and
Matt Hughes? You knew what they were
going to try and do in the cage before
they entered it. And Dakota's identity
is not complicated. She wants to hurt
you everywhere the referee still allows.

the right road around her.
