This is Rummy’s Corner.
Oleksandr Usyk sure did make a mess of  
thighs when he recently shook up the heavyweight 
landscape! We are in uncharted territory! Indeed,  
it’s like the wild west out there! 
It all started when Usyk decided to vacate the  
remaining 3 belts from his unified collection. 
Usyk had technically twice been undisputed at  
heavyweight, which was a technicality, and also 
ironic given that Usyk never once defended the  
undisputed crown. Now Usyk tossed the alphabet 
trinkets, despite also announcing he would NOT  
be retiring. This is unprecedented - never in 
the modern alphabet history of the sport has  
a heavyweight champion dumped 3 belts at once - 
without retiring. All the same, Usyk remains the  
one and only true lineal world heavyweight 
champion for as long as he’s active, making  
the rest of the alphabet belts a complete and 
total joke. Case in point, Daniel Dubois is the  
reigning WBO champion, and he was dominated and 
knocked out by Usyk in both of their fights. 
 
Agit Kabayel was Usyk’s upcoming WBC mandatory, 
and he has now been promoted as the WBC  
Real McCoy. The IBF remains vacant, but the 
so-called “regular” WBA champion, Murat Gassiev,  
got promoted as the real WBA champion - which is 
another joke, really. No disrespect to Gassiev,  
but the fact that the WBA “regular” belt even 
still exists is a huge joke in its own right.  
Of course, Usyk had the real belt, the WBA 
super duper deluxe. But now Gassiev is the  
WBA belt holder, the same guy who was outboxed 
and beaten by Otto Wallin a few years back,  
which is not THAT long ago based on modern 
heavyweight activity. Gassiev has another  
loss to Usyk, albeit at cruiserweight, but you 
get the picture. 2 of the 3 alphabet titlists  
have already lost to Usyk, making their belts 
utterly worthless - at least for as long as  
Usyk is fighting professionally. And that’s true 
of the other two as well. Make no mistake - Usyk  
is the one and only real heavyweight champion!
Before Usyk dumped the belts, Gassiev was supposed  
to defend his minor WBA regular trinket against 
Tony Yoka, but then Usyk shook up the world,  
and then Yoka pulled out with injury. For a brief 
moment, it looked like Juggernaut Joe Joyce might  
step in as a late replacement. Even though he 
lost 4 of his last 5, he is at least a known name,  
but he must not be paying a vig to the WBA 
at this time, because they rejected him,  
but they gladly accepted Peter Kadiru. Guess 
which one IS paying the WBA a vig, via something  
called the WBA Continental Europe Title. 
So here we are, and we have a heavyweight title  
fight between Gassiev - a man who has beaten 
no one of note at heavyweight, and Kadiru,  
a man most boxing fans probably never even 
heard of. It’s a very bizarre situation  
that Usyk has brought before us following his 
tougher than expected victory in the crossover  
fight against kickboxer Rico Verhoeven. 
The only reason Gassiev even holds that WBA  
belt is because he beat 45 year old Kubrat Pulev 
last December - a man who had been stopped by the  
great Wladimir Klitschko more than a dozen 
years ago! You cannot make this stuff up.  
Pulev was the WBA regular champion, despite being 
a non-factor in the heavyweight division since he  
was brutally stopped against AJ in his last 
bid for a real, major world title. And then  
Kadiru is coming off a 10 round unanimous decision 
victory against Senad Gashi - although it should  
probably be said that, even though that fight 
was later framed as a clear, disciplined victory,  
there are a number of vocal dissenting voices out 
there who believe Gashi got robbed in Germany.
Bottom line, this has to be one of the more 
obscure heavyweight title fights in quite a  
long while, where neither the champion nor his 
challenger is held in especially high esteem  
by the boxing world at large. That being 
said, Gassiev is the more proven talent,  
plain and simple, and his skill and power 
should be enough to get Kadiru out of there,  
probably sooner than later.
So for my official prediction,  
I am going with Gassiev by 2nd round knockout. I 
think his power should shine here and that there  
is a clear gap in terms of skills, but as always - 
what the hell do I know? I ain’t exactly Quasimodo  
over here. And even though this isn’t a match-up 
that I was ever especially interested in seeing,  
it is a heavyweight fight with a major world 
title on the line, which can be viewed as the  
first chapter following Usyk’s decision 
to dump his remaining alphabet belts,  
which will all remain completely worthless 
for as long as Usyk is actively competing.
