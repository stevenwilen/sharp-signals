When the pace is clean and the cage is
spacious, Gan looks like a heavyweight
out of a video
game. Footwork, range, movements, all
flawless. When he danced around Taiu
Ivasa or picked apart Derrick Lewis, it
didn't even look like they were in the
same sport. He's a big, powerful, badass
heavyweight who moves like a
middleweight. Alderman Sterling, too.
When he controls the tempo, gets the
back, and starts racking up control
time. It's like watching a cardio
machine in motion when he's not
pressured. When the fight stays in his
structure, he thrives. But that's the
catch, isn't it? Look at the face of
Leon Edwards after round four in the
first Usman fight. Or Gon's face after
being brutally bullied by Jon Jones.
Think about what happened when
Sterling's takedown stopped working in
the first Yan fight. That's not just
fatigue, that's frustration.
