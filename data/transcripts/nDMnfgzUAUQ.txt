The UFC has never been more profitable,
but has it ever felt more soulless? You
can feel it in the silence between the
fights. The walkouts still look
dramatic. The gloves still touch, but
the energy is different, manufactured,
and hollow. Every event used to feel
like a collision course, a culmination
of robberies, storylines, and momentum.
Now though, half the card is filled with
names you don't know, fighting for
reasons you don't care about. And the
ones you do know, they're either being
kept on the shelf for the right moment
or getting tossed into matchups that
feel more algorithmic than earned. The
soul of MMA has always been its rawness,
its unpredictability, its purity. That
sense that anything could happen and
every fight meant something. But when
fights are booked to fill slot instead
of settle scores, when contenders are
ignored in favor of fading stars, when
title shots are handed out based on
social media impressions, it stops
feeling like a sport.
